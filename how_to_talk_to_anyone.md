# How To Talk To Anyone

92 tips/tricks from Leil Lowndes

## Part 1: How to Intrigue Everyone Without Saying a Word

First impressions happen in seconds, often before words are spoken. This section shows how to use
body language, posture, eye contact, and subtle gestures to project confidence, warmth, and
credibility right from the start.  

1. The Flooding Smile - Smile warmly, but delay a second so it seems genuine.  
2. Sticky Eyes - Maintain strong eye contact slightly longer than usual.  
3. Epoxy Eyes - Keep eyes on your target even when others are speaking.  
4. Hang by Your Teeth - Visualize being lifted by your teeth for confident posture.  
5. The Big-Baby Pivot - Turn your body fully toward new people as if they are special.  
6. Hello Old Friend - Pretend each new person is a long-lost friend.  
7. Limit the Fidget - Eliminate nervous movements to appear credible.  
8. Hans's Horse Sense - Watch subtle body language cues and adapt.  
9. Watch the Scene Before You Make the Scene - Visualize success before entering.  

## Part 2: How to Know What to Say After You Say Hi

Small talk can feel awkward, but it is the bridge to deeper connections. This section teaches how to
avoid boring answers, pick up on conversational clues, and use curiosity to keep conversations
flowing.  

10. Make a Mood Match - Match the emotional tone of the other person.  
11. Prosaic with Passion - Speak about simple things with energy.  
12. Always Wear a Whatzit - Carry something unusual to spark conversation.  
13. Whoozat? - Ask about a third party to keep conversation going.  
14. Eavesdrop In - Listen nearby and join conversations with relevance.  
15. Never the Naked City - Give interesting, specific answers to "Where are you from?"  
16. Never the Naked Job - Describe your job in a memorable, intriguing way.  
17. Never the Naked Introduction - Add a hook when introducing others.  
18. Be a Word Detective - Pick up unique words others use and mirror them.  
19. The Swiveling Spotlight - Shift attention away from yourself to the other person.  
20. Parroting - Repeat the last few words they say to encourage elaboration.  
21. Encore! - Ask people to tell a good story again.  

## Part 3: How to Talk Like the VIPs Do

Successful people sound interesting because they prepare material, use smart compliments, and mix in
current events. This section shows how to sound engaging and memorable, not dull.  

22. Never the Naked Thank You - Add a specific reason to your thanks.  
23. Scramble Therapy - Shake up routines to gather fresh conversation material.  
24. Learn a Little Jobbledygook - Know a few insider words from many fields.  
25. Baring Their Hot Button - Identify and speak about what excites them.  
26. The Killer Compliment - Give sincere, memorable compliments.  
27. The Latest News...Don't Leave Home Without It - Keep up with current events.  
28. Beware of the Naked List - Do not rattle off facts; weave them into stories.  

## Part 4: How to Be an Insider in Any Crowd

Every group has its own customs, language, and culture. This section helps you fit in quickly by
picking up their lingo, rituals, and ways of thinking so you appear as an insider.  

29. Read Their Rags - Skim industry publications to speak their lingo.  
30. Clear "Customs" - Learn the rituals of their group before entering.  
31. See the Big Picture - Understand overall issues before details.  
32. Learn Their Pet Words - Use the insider phrases of the group.  
33. The Grapevine Glory - Share news that has not yet gone mainstream.  
34. Accentuate the Positive - Avoid complaints; emphasize good news.  

## Part 5: How to Sound Like You Have a Super Personality

Charisma comes from showing enthusiasm, empathy, and a sense of connection. This section teaches
small verbal and nonverbal cues that make you seem warm, likable, and engaging.  

35. The "Oh Wow, It's You!" Reaction - Greet people as if they are special.  
36. The Premature "We" - Use "we" early to build rapport.  
37. Instant History - Create shared memories quickly ("This reminds me of...").  
38. The Futurezamine Technique - Envision future connections with them.  
39. Nod Your Head Yes - Subtle nodding builds agreement.  
40. The Knee-Jerk "Wow!" - Use small exclamations to validate others.  
41. Employ Empathizers - Say phrases like "I understand how you feel."  

## Part 6: How to Differentiate the Power of Praise

Compliments are powerful but must be unique and sincere. This section shows how to avoid cliches,
make praise personal, and deliver it in ways that stick.  

42. Kill with a Compliment - Praise something specific and unique.  
43. Comm-YOU-nication - Focus praise on the other person, not yourself.  
44. The Exclusive Smile - Smile as if it is only for them.  
45. Don't Touch a Cliche with a 10-Foot Pole - Avoid overused lines.  
46. Use Personalized Praise - Link compliments to their identity.  

## Part 7: How to Direct Dial Their Hearts

Tone and phrasing can create intimacy and trust. This section introduces subtle techniques like
whispering, reflecting emotions, and repetition to build deeper bonds.  

47. The Whisper Technique - Lower your voice for intimacy.  
48. Call a Spade a Spade - Be direct when necessary.  
49. The Broken Record - Repeat your point calmly until acknowledged.  
50. Echoing Emotions - Reflect their feelings back to them.  

## Part 8: How to Work a Party Like a Politician

Mingling at social events requires confidence and strategy. This section shows how to approach
groups, use body language, and handle first impressions in party settings.  

51. Munching or Mingling - Avoid being stuck with food/drink in both hands.  
52. Rubberneck the Room - Scan, then move confidently.  
53. The Tracking Technique - Introduce yourself smoothly into groups.  
54. Be the Chooser, Not the Choosee - Take initiative approaching others.  
55. Come-Hither Hands - Keep palms visible and open.  
56. The Crocodile Smile - Smile sincerely, not mechanically.  

## Part 9: How to Break the Most Treacherous Glass Ceiling of All

Long-term relationships are built on recognition and memory. This section focuses on names,
compliments, and remembering details to make people feel valued.  

57. The "Boy Oh Boy, Are You Something!" Compliment - Special recognition.  
58. The Tombstone Game - Ask what they want remembered for.  
59. The Broken Record on Names - Use names frequently.  
60. The Politician's Trick - Remember small personal details.  

## Part 10: How to Get What You Want from Anyone

Persuasion requires calm persistence and fairness. This section gives tools for staying composed,
aligning emotionally, and gently but firmly pushing your agenda.  

61. Don't Frown, Don't Pout - Keep emotions in check.  
62. Watch the Scene Before You Make the Scene (Reapplied) - Visualize negotiation.  
63. The Sneaky Sympathy - Subtly align with their struggles.  
64. The Great Scorecard in the Sky - Appeal to fairness.  
65. The Conversational Join-In - Add to what is said, do not one-up.  
66. Be a Broken Record (in Persuasion) - Calmly stick to your ask.  

## Part 11: How to Make Them Want to See You Again

Endings matter as much as beginnings. This section shows how to leave on a positive note, set up the
next meeting, and stay memorable.  

67. The "Good-Bye Is Never Forever" Technique - Leave with a plan to meet again.  
68. The Best First Date Ever - Make the focus entirely on them.  
69. The Business Card Dossier - Write notes about people for next time.  
70. The Never the Naked Good-Bye - Add warmth and personalization.  

## Part 12: Little Tricks for Big Success

The final section offers catch-all tips for lasting influence, from handshakes to humor. These
techniques keep you likable, memorable, and welcomed back.  

71. The Broken Record of Praise - Reuse a great compliment.  
72. Don't Leave Without a Word - Always say proper farewells.  
73. The Eyeball Sell - Read people's subtle signals in persuasion.  
74. Be the First to Congratulate - Celebrate even small wins.  
75. The "Exclusive" Invitation - Make people feel chosen.  
76. Be a VIP with Everyone - Treat all people as if they matter.  
77. The Killer Handshake - Firm, confident, but not overbearing.  
78. The Perfect Timing Compliment - Deliver praise when it counts.  
79. Be a Copycat (Subtly) - Mirror behavior to build trust.  
80. The "Sneaky Compliment" - Praise indirectly through a third party.  
81. Be a Devil's Advocate - Disagree lightly to stimulate thinking.  
82. The Exclusive "We" - Build insider connection with language.  
83. Keep the Mystery Alive - Do not reveal everything at once.  
84. Never Boast Naked - Wrap self-praise in a story.  
85. The Humble-Brag Done Right - Share indirectly through others.  
86. Let Them Be the Expert - Ask their advice or knowledge.  
87. The Last Laugh Technique - End conversations on a humorous note.  
88. Plant a "Compliment Bomb" - Praise them to others who will tell them.  
89. Keep Them Curious - Hint at stories but do not finish them.  
90. The "Naked" Compliment Reminder - Never generic; always unique.  
91. The Second-Half Smile - Smile again when leaving.  
92. Always Leave Them Wanting More - End interactions while energy is high.
